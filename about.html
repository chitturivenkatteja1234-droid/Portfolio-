About

Six years into building for the web, with a growing focus on the parts most sites skip.

I started out designing marketing pages, and got pulled toward accessibility work after watching someone navigate a site I'd built entirely by keyboard — and getting stuck. Since then, semantic structure and assistive-tech support have been the first thing I design for, not the last thing I patch in.

Outside of client work I write about markup patterns, review pull requests for a local accessibility meetup, and keep a running list of the ARIA mistakes I've made so I stop repeating them.
